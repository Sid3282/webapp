About Nepali Calendar (Bikram Sambat)

The Nepali calendar, also known as Bikram Sambat (BS), is the official calendar of Nepal. It is approximately 56.7 years ahead of the Gregorian calendar (AD). The new year begins in mid-April (Baisakh 1).

Key Features of Bikram Sambat:

  • Based on ancient Hindu calendar system
  • Starts from 57 BCE (year 0 in BS = 57 BCE in Gregorian)
  • Lunar-solar calendar (months based on moon, years based on sun)
  • 12 months with varying days (29-32 days)

Important Nepali Months:

  • Baisakh (April-May): New Year, start of fiscal year
  • Ashad (June-July): Start of monsoon, rice planting
  • Kartik (October-November): Festival season starts
  • Falgun (February-March): Spring season, Holi festival

Major Nepali Festivals:

  • Dashain (Sept-Oct): Biggest Hindu festival (15 days)
  • Tihar (Oct-Nov): Festival of lights (5 days)
  • Chhath (Oct-Nov): Sun worship festival
  • Holi (March): Festival of colors

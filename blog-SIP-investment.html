SIP in Nepal: A Complete Guide to Systematic Investment Plans

June 10, 2023 Siddh Batala 8 min read

Systematic Investment Plans (SIPs) have become increasingly popular in Nepal as a disciplined approach to investing in mutual funds. This guide will explain everything you need to know about SIP investments in Nepal, including how they work, their benefits, risks, and how to get started.

What is SIP?

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is an investment strategy where you invest a fixed amount of money at regular intervals (monthly, quarterly, etc.) in a mutual fund scheme. Instead of investing a lump sum amount at once, SIP allows you to invest smaller amounts periodically.

Key Concept: SIP follows the principle of rupee cost averaging, which means you buy more units when prices are low and fewer units when prices are high, potentially reducing your average cost per unit over time.

How SIP Works in Nepal

In Nepal, SIP works similarly to other countries but with some local considerations:

  1. Choose a Mutual Fund: Select from various mutual fund schemes offered by licensed asset management companies in Nepal.
  2. Decide Investment Amount: Determine how much you can invest regularly (minimum amounts vary by fund).
  3. Select Frequency: Typically monthly, but some funds offer quarterly options.
  4. Set Up Auto-Debit: Link your bank account for automatic payments.
  5. Monitor and Adjust: Track performance and adjust as needed.

Benefits of SIP in Nepal

1. Disciplined Investing

SIP enforces financial discipline by making investing a regular habit, which is particularly beneficial for Nepali investors who might otherwise spend rather than save.

2. Affordable Entry Point

Many SIPs in Nepal start with as little as NPR 1,000 per month, making them accessible to a wide range of investors.

3. Rupee Cost Averaging

This strategy helps mitigate the impact of market volatility, which can be significant in Nepal's developing market.

4. Power of Compounding

Long-term SIP investments benefit from compounding, where your returns generate their own returns over time.

5. Flexibility

Most SIPs in Nepal allow you to increase, decrease, pause, or stop your investments as needed.

SIP vs Lump Sum Investment

Feature SIP Lump Sum
Investment Style Regular, small amounts One-time large amount
Market Timing Not critical (averages cost) Critical (need good timing)
Risk Lower (spreads risk over time) Higher (exposed to market timing)
Discipline Enforces saving habit Requires self-discipline
Best For Salaried individuals, beginners Those with large sums, experienced

How to Start SIP in Nepal

1. Choose the Right Fund

Consider your risk appetite and investment goals. Equity funds offer higher potential returns but with higher risk, while debt funds are more stable but with lower returns.

2. Complete KYC

You'll need to complete Know Your Customer (KYC) formalities with the asset management company, which typically requires:

  • Citizenship certificate copy
  • Recent photograph
  • Bank account details

3. Set Up Auto-Debit

Most companies require you to sign a standing instruction for auto-debit from your bank account.

4. Monitor Your Investments

Regularly review your SIP performance and adjust your strategy as needed based on market conditions and personal financial goals.

Tax Implications of SIP in Nepal

As of 2023, the tax treatment of mutual fund investments in Nepal is as follows:

  • Dividends: Tax-free in the hands of investors
  • Capital Gains:
    • Held less than 1 year: 7.5%
    • Held 1-3 years: 5%
    • Held more than 3 years: No tax

Always consult with a tax professional for the most current information.

Risks and Considerations

1. Market Risk

Mutual funds are subject to market fluctuations, especially equity-oriented funds.

2. Inflation Risk

Returns may not always outpace inflation, especially with debt funds.

3. Liquidity

While mutual funds are generally liquid, some funds may have exit loads or processing times.

4. Fund Performance

Past performance doesn't guarantee future results. Research the fund's track record and management.

Tip: Diversify your SIP investments across different types of funds to spread risk. Consider a mix of equity, debt, and balanced funds based on your risk tolerance.

Conclusion

SIP is an excellent investment vehicle for Nepali investors looking to build wealth systematically. With options starting from just NPR 1,000 per month, it's accessible to most income groups. The key to successful SIP investing is starting early, staying consistent, and maintaining a long-term perspective.

Remember that all investments carry risk, and it's important to choose funds that align with your financial goals and risk tolerance. Consider consulting with a financial advisor before making investment decisions.

Gold Trading in Nepal: What You Need to Know

By Siddha Batala Posted on April 28, 2023 7 min read

Gold holds a special place in Nepali culture, serving as both a traditional investment and an essential part of ceremonies and festivals. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about gold prices, trading practices, and investment strategies in Nepal's unique market.

Understanding Gold Prices in Nepal

Gold prices in Nepal are influenced by several factors:

  • International gold prices: Nepal's prices closely follow global market trends
  • USD/NPR exchange rate: Since gold is traded in USD internationally
  • Government taxes: Import duties and VAT affect final prices
  • Local demand: Prices rise during festival seasons

Pro Tip:

The best time to buy gold in Nepal is typically during the monsoon season (June-August) when demand is lower, while prices often peak during festival seasons (September-November).

Where to Buy Gold in Nepal

1. Licensed Jewelers

Reputable jewelers in major cities like:

  • New Road area in Kathmandu
  • Pokhara's Lakeside market
  • Birgunj and Biratnagar markets

2. Banks

Several Nepali banks now offer gold coins and bars:

  • Nepal Rastra Bank (central bank)
  • Commercial banks with precious metal licenses

3. Online Platforms

Emerging digital gold platforms (verify legitimacy before purchasing)

Gold Purity Standards in Nepal

International

  • Measured in karats (24K = pure gold)
  • 24K = 99.9% pure
  • 22K = 91.6% pure
  • 18K = 75% pure
  • 14K = 58.3% pure

Nepali Terms

  • "Saat ratti" = 22K (91.6% pure)
  • "Chha ratti" = 18K (75% pure)
  • Traditional "Tejabi" gold ≈ 20-21K
  • Hallmarking becoming more common

Legal Regulations

Nepal has specific rules governing gold trade:

  • Individuals can carry up to 50g of gold when entering Nepal
  • Commercial imports require special licenses
  • All gold must be declared at customs
  • Jewelers must maintain proper records of transactions

Investment Considerations

Pros of Gold Investment

  • Hedge against inflation and currency fluctuations
  • High liquidity in Nepali market
  • Cultural acceptance as store of value
  • No capital gains tax on personal gold holdings

Cons to Consider

  • No passive income (unlike rental property or dividends)
  • Storage and security concerns
  • Making charges reduce value when buying jewelry
  • Price volatility in short term

Tips for First-Time Buyers

  1. Always ask for a proper invoice with purity details
  2. Check for BIS hallmark or other certification
  3. Compare prices across 3-4 reputable jewelers
  4. Understand making charges (typically 10-15% of gold value)
  5. Consider insurance for valuable pieces

Smart Buying:

When buying jewelry, focus on designs with minimal wastage that can be easily melted and remade if needed. Simple chains and bangles often have better resale value than intricate pieces.

Future Trends

The gold market in Nepal is evolving with:

  • Increased digital gold investment options
  • More stringent purity regulations
  • Growing interest from younger investors
  • Potential for gold-backed financial products

Conclusion

Gold remains one of the most trusted investments in Nepal, deeply rooted in culture while serving practical financial purposes. Whether you're buying for personal use, as a gift, or as part of your investment portfolio, understanding the market dynamics will help you make informed decisions. Always stay updated with current prices and regulations, and consider diversifying your holdings beyond physical gold.
